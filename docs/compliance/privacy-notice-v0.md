## How this clinic uses your health information

**Who is responsible for your information?**
This clinic is responsible for your health record. The clinic uses a secure computer
system called **Sankofa EHR**, run by [OPERATOR LEGAL NAME], which stores the
information safely on the clinic's behalf. Both the clinic and the system operator
follow Ghana's Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) and are registered with the Data
Protection Commission. *(Registration numbers to be inserted once issued.)*

**What information do we collect?**
- Your name, date of birth, sex, address, and phone number.
- Your Ghana Card number and NHIS number, if you have them.
- Your health information: symptoms, test results, diagnoses, medicines, vaccinations,
  and pregnancy care.
- Payment records for your visits.

**Why do we collect it?**
- To care for you safely — your nurse, doctor, lab, and pharmacy see what they need to
  treat you.
- To bill correctly and, if you use NHIS, to prepare your insurance claim.
- To keep the records the law requires health facilities to keep.

**Sharing with other clinics in the network**
This clinic is part of a network of clinics that use the same system. If you visit
another network clinic, that clinic can only see your record **if you agree** when you
are there. Staff at other clinics cannot browse your record without your consent. In a
medical emergency where you cannot consent, a clinician may open your record using a
special emergency procedure — every such access is recorded and checked.

**Who else sees your information?**
Nobody else, except when the law requires it (for example, public-health reporting) or
you ask us to share it (for example, a referral letter). We do not sell your
information or use it for advertising.

**How long do we keep it?**
Health records are kept long-term because they may be needed for your future care.
We do not delete health records without a lawful reason.

**Your rights under Act 843**
- **Access:** you can ask to see the information we hold about you and receive a copy
  (a small administrative fee may apply).
- **Correction:** if something is wrong, you can ask us to correct it.
- **Consent choices:** you can refuse or later withdraw consent for other network
  clinics to see your record, and opt out of SMS reminders at any time.
- **Complaint:** if you are not satisfied with how your information is handled, you may
  complain to the Data Protection Commission (dataprotection.org.gh).

**How to contact us**
Speak to the front desk or the clinic administrator at this clinic. They will handle
your request or pass it to the system operator when needed.
